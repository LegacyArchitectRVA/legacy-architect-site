# Legacy Architect RVA Website Design Direction

## Target feel

The site should feel futuristic, but not sci-fi.

That means modern, quiet, precise, spacious, and systemized. It should not use neon effects, space imagery, cyberpunk gradients, holograms, robot language, or anything theatrical.

The visual standard is calm technology, organized life infrastructure, and executive restraint.

## Design principles

1. Clarity first. Every section should answer one question and move the visitor one step forward.
2. Quiet depth. Use layered cards, soft borders, subtle shadows, and controlled contrast.
3. System language. Use clean grids, structured modules, numbered steps, and organized content blocks.
4. Human restraint. The site can feel advanced, but it should still feel personal, private, and grounded.
5. No drama. Avoid urgency, fear-based phrasing, countdown language, alarmist statistics, or visual pressure.

## Visual direction

Preferred:
- Warm off-white or deep charcoal backgrounds
- Soft neutral panels
- Subtle blue-gray or muted gold accent
- Thin lines
- Rounded cards
- Generous spacing
- Clean typography
- Modular page sections
- Light motion only if it improves comprehension

Avoid:
- Neon colors
- Sci-fi imagery
- Space imagery
- Futuristic cliches
- Glowing buttons
- Digital rain effects
- Robot or AI language
- Excessive animation
- Overly technical visuals

## Homepage layout direction

1. Hero: large calm headline, minimal copy, one primary action.
2. Operational problem: life information is scattered across accounts, folders, devices, routines, and memory.
3. Life Manual explanation: position it as the operational how, distinct from wills and trusts.
4. Process: Preserve. Confirm authority. Organize. Deliver. Delete.
5. Service path: Workbook, Blueprint Session, Life Manual packages.
6. Privacy standard: no credential storage, client controls Secure Drive, clear boundaries.
7. Testimonial: one restrained testimonial block.
8. Final action: simple invitation to begin.

## Copy tone

Plain spoken, calm, and exact.

Use:
- Facilitate
- Help create
- Help organize

Avoid:
- Advise
- Consult
- Recommend
- Legal guidance
- Financial guidance
- Fear language
- Urgency language
